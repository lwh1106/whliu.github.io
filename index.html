About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Climate and Space Sciences and Engineering at the University of Michigan, with Ph.D. training in Scientific Computing. My research focuses on solar and heliospheric physics, solar energetic particles, galactic cosmic rays, and particle radiation transport.

I develop physics-based and data-informed computational tools for space-weather applications, including high-performance modeling workflows for SEP forecasting and radiation environment analysis.

Research Interests

  • Solar energetic particle acceleration, transport, prediction, and event benchmarking.
  • Galactic cosmic rays, radiation dose modeling, and Earth-Mars travel radiation environments.
  • Scientific computing for heliophysics: Fortran, Python, MPI, OpenMP, PETSc, SLURM, and model automation.
  • Machine learning and data-driven methods for space-weather forecasting workflows.
